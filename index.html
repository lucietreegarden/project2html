Flora of New York State

Secret of New York State Flower's

Gardening is a hobby enjoyed by many, young and old. If you asked any experienced gardener what the hardest part of gardening would be, you would probably get several answers. The weather, keeping up with watering or pests destroying your hard grown veggies.

A common issue however, would be the care of different plants with their different quirks and needs. Every gardener could name at least one plant in their collection that causes them constant trouble. They are easily over-watered or under-watered, they get too much sun or too little, in general they have a weak constitution. Plants and flowers that are not indigenous to the area in which you are gardening will certainly face bigger challenges than the native species.

A good way to avoid finicky plants is to majority, or completely, plant native species. The real secret to New York state plants is that native plants require less care than other non-native ornamental plants.

These flowers are already well established, and do not require any special care or additional nutrients.

There are many benefits to creating a garden space with native flora in it. The growing of native flowers provide protection and food for bees, birds and other native wildlife. Bees especially benefit from a healthy varied flower garden. The different shapes and sizes from Joe Pye and Bee Balm to Coneflowers and Goldenrod provide a healthy base for your backyard ecosystem.

Flowers indigenous to New York will also bloom throughout the year when there is no frost. In the spring we get Lilies of the Valley, in summer we get Daisies, and in the fall we get Goldenrod. If you would like to create a beautiful space that also helps nourish your backyard's ecosystem consider planting mostly indigenous New York State flowers, the bees and other wildlife will thank you.
